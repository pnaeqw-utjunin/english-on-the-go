4. GRAMMAR – Future forms: present continuous and be going to

Let’s continue exploring how to talk about the future. There are several ways in English, but today we’ll focus on be going to and present continuous for future plans. – Continuemos explorando cómo hablar del futuro. Hay varias formas en inglés, pero hoy nos centraremos en "be going to" y el presente continuo para planes futuros.

Yes! We use be going to to talk about things we intend to do or have already decided. – Sí. Usamos "be going to" para hablar de cosas que tenemos la intención de hacer o que ya hemos decidido.

For example:
  • I’m going to call my brother tonight – Voy a llamar a mi hermano esta noche.
  • She’s going to study abroad next year – Ella va a estudiar en el extranjero el próximo año.

And we use present continuous for fixed arrangements, something we’ve already organized. – Y usamos el presente continuo para arreglos fijos, algo que ya hemos organizado.
  • I’m meeting my parents for lunch tomorrow – Me reuniré con mis padres para almorzar mañana.
  • We’re visiting our grandparents on Sunday – Visitaremos a nuestros abuelos el domingo.

Notice how both tenses talk about the future, but the feeling is slightly different.
“Be going to” = plan or intention.
“Present continuous” = confirmed arrangement. – "Be going to" = plan o intención. "Present continuous" = arreglo confirmado.

Let’s try a quick mini-quiz! I’ll say a sentence, and you decide if it’s be going to or present continuous.
Number one: We’re having dinner with my uncle tonight. – Número uno: Vamos a cenar con mi tío esta noche.
That’s present continuous – it’s an arrangement. – Es presente continuo - es un arreglo.

Number two: I’m going to buy a gift for my mom. – Número dos: Voy a comprar un regalo para mi mamá.
That’s be going to, because it’s a plan. – Es "be going to" porque es un plan.

Well done! Keep practicing both forms to sound natural when you talk about the future. – ¡Bien hecho! Sigue practicando ambas formas para sonar natural cuando hables del futuro.

5. PRONUNCIATION & SPEAKING – Sentence stress

Now, pronunciation time! In English, some words in a sentence are stressed, and others are not. – ¡Hora de la pronunciación! En inglés, algunas palabras en una oración se acentúan y otras no.

Yes. We usually stress content words — the ones that carry meaning: nouns, verbs, adjectives, and adverbs. – Sí. Usualmente acentuamos las palabras de contenido — aquellas que llevan significado: sustantivos, verbos, adjetivos y adverbios.

Let’s listen to a sentence: I’m going to look for a job – Voy a buscar trabajo.
Which words are stressed?
The stressed words are going, look, and job. We don’t stress I’m or to.

Exactly! Listen and repeat with the right rhythm:
  • I’m going to look for a job.
  • We’re meeting our cousins tomorrow – Nos reuniremos con nuestros primos mañana.
  • She’s going to start a new course – Ella va a comenzar un nuevo curso.
  • They’re traveling abroad this month – Ellos viajarán al extranjero este mes.

Excellent rhythm! Paying attention to stress makes your English sound more natural and easier to understand. – ¡Excelente ritmo! Prestar atención al acento hace que tu inglés suene más natural y fácil de entender.

The oldest child often looks after younger brothers and sisters. They’re usually sensible and responsible, and they like to be leaders. However, they can sometimes be anxious or perfectionist.

The middle child tends to be sociable and relaxed. They’re good at solving conflicts and often act as peacemakers. But sometimes they feel ignored and lack direction.

The youngest child is often outgoing and funny. They get a lot of attention from parents, so they can become charming but also a little spoiled.

The only child usually spends more time with adults. They’re independent, organized, and sometimes a bit selfish or impatient. – Hoy en día se acepta que nuestra posición en la familia afecta fuertemente quiénes somos. El hijo del medio tiende a ser sociable y relajado. Son buenos resolviendo conflictos y frecuentemente actúan como mediadores. Pero a veces se sienten ignorados y les falta dirección.
